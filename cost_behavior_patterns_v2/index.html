Based on: Horngren, Charles T.; Datar, Srikant M.; Rajan, Madhav: Cost accounting: a managerial emphasis, 14th edition 2012.
  • Leasing fee for a production plant provided by the government, for which the contract calls for a fixed fee unless 100,000 labor hours are worked, in which case no fee is paid. Waste collection charges – a fixed fee, plus a variable cost after a certain number of waste tons are produced. The tons of waste vary proportionally with the number of units produced.
  • Electricity bill, which is computed as follows:
    · First 1,000 kWh or less: Flat fee of $200
    · Next 1,000 kWh: $0.20 per KWh used
    · Next 1,000 kWh: $0.40 per KWh used
    · Next 1,000 kWh: $0.60 per KWh used
  • Direct material costs. Direct material costs per unit are constant at $5 if the company buys less than 200 kilograms. For each kg the company purchases above 200 kg, it receives a discount on the price per kg such that each additional kg of material costs $4.
  • Salaries of maintenance personnel. One person is needed for every 500 machine hours or less. Hence, one person is needed for 0 to 500 hours, two persons are needed for 501 to 1,000 hours, and so on. Leasing fee for a production plant provided by the city, for which the contract calls for a fixed fee of $250,000 that is reduced by $10 for each manufacturing labor hour worked more than 200,000 hours. In any case, the firm must pay a rental fee of $50,000.
